## Why People Buy a Virtual Card with Crypto in the First Place

Before getting into any specific brand, it's worth understanding what you're actually buying. A crypto virtual card is essentially a prepaid Visa or Mastercard that you load with cryptocurrency instead of fiat. When you make a purchase, the platform converts your crypto balance into the merchant's currency in the background, and the merchant just sees a normal card transaction.

People reach for this solution for a few recurring reasons:

- **Subscriptions that reject their cards** — streaming services, AI tools, SaaS products, and ad platforms are notoriously picky about which cards they accept, especially if you're outside the US or EU.
- **No easy access to international cards** — not everyone can get a USD or EUR denominated bank card through their local financial system.
- **Spending crypto without off-ramping through an exchange** — converting USDT to fiat and wiring it to a bank account can take days and trigger questions. A virtual card skips that.
- **Privacy and separation** — some users prefer not to expose their primary bank card on every random website they buy from.

The trade-off is that crypto cards are rarely free. There are activation fees, top-up fees, transaction fees, FX fees, and sometimes cross-border fees stacked on top. Knowing how those fees stack is the difference between a card that saves you money and one that quietly bleeds it.
